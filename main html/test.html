Samurai

Samurai were the hereditary military nobility and officer caste of medieval and early-modern Japan from the 12th century to their abolition in the 1870s.
They were the well-paid retainers of the daimyo. They had high prestige and special privileges such as wearing two swords.

Yokai

Yōkai (妖怪, "ghost", "phantom", "strange apparition") are a class of supernatural monsters and spirits in Japanese folklore.
The characteristics of yōkai range diversely from malevolent and mischievous entities believed to cause misfortune and harm, to those who are considered to bring good fortune to those who encounter them.
